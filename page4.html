How to Share Images and Video to Facebook

Sharing images and video through facebook posts

As most of you know, Facebook has recently changed it's policy when sharing links to your pages and timelines. You can no longer change the link image. So when you copy and paste the link into your status update, you're stuck with whatever image populates. Here's a way to get around this!

First of all, type in your caption (or copy it from our pre-written copy here).

Once you enter a URL into the draft of your post, Facebook will populate a link and clickable image...automatically. But you don't want that. Hover over the link that is created, and press the "X" in the top right corner. That will delete the clickable link. But never fear, people can still click the link in your caption to get to your destination.
Next you want to add the image.
Choose the option:
Upload Photos/Video: Add photos or a video from your computer. The photos you publish will be added to your Page's Timeline Photos album.
Select the photos or videos you want to add
Click Publish

SHARING IMAGES AND VIDEO THROUGH FACEBOOK STORY

Facebook Story sharing is only available on your personal accounts. Right now, you can't share a Facebook Story from you business page. Sad day. :(

1. Login to your updated app, as soon as you do you will notice the Stories Bar. It’s located at the top of your news feed.
2. To access its features, you have two choices; you can either tap the Your Stories Icon or the Camera Icon in the top-left corner of the screen.
3. Next, you will be presented with a which looks similar to what you may have seen on Instagram. At the bottom-center in a large Circular Button, tapping it will take a photo, holding it will shot video.
However, if you want to share something already stored on your device, you can via the icon on the bottom-right. Once selecting this you will be shown your images/video starting with the most recent. You can scroll through those shown from left-to-right, just tap to select.
4. Having now created or found the content for your first voyage into Facebook Stories it’s now time to save it. This can be done by tapping the large Circular Arrow Button which has appeared at the bottom of the screen.
5. ON the following screen you will see Your Story with a blue tick next to it. Looking at the bottom of the screen you will see a Blue Button. Tapping it will add Your Story to the Facebook feed for all your friends to see.

Additionally, you can select friends from the list who you would like to give one more view. This special viewing will allow them a window of 24 hours after that it will be gone.

How To Use Facebook Stories effects

Using Camera Effects

If you want to add effects to your Facebook Stories before posting, you can do so. However, from Step 3, do either of the following before saving.
1. There are two effects icons on the screen. One adds frames, masks, or camera effects. It can be found at the bottom-left of the app.
2. The next icon allows you to doodle over your image, it is the Downward Facing Pencil. Selecting it will reveal a selection of colors for you to choose.

Once you have selected and added your chosen effects, continue from Step 4 above to save and share your very first Facebook Story.

HOW TO CHANGE YOUR COVER PHOTO

A cover photo is the larger photo at the top of your profile, above your profile picture. Like your profile picture, cover photos are public, which means anyone visiting your profile will be able to see them. We've provided a series of images to choose from to promote the report! They're on the main a

To add or change your cover photo:
Go to your profile.
Hover over Update Cover Photo in the top-left of your current cover photo.
Click Update Cover Photo. If you don't currently have a cover photo, click Add Cover Photo.
Choose whether you want to upload a new photo or pick a photo from one of your existing photo albums.
Once you choose a photo, you can reposition it by clicking the image and dragging it up or down.
Click Save Changes.
Note: Your cover image has to be at least 720 pixels wide.
